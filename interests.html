Security

Cybersecurity

Data breaches, malware, and other attacks can put our personal information in danger. Every year, there are many security compromises, which leads to distrust and stolen information which can potentially be used for crime.

My primary interests include: encryption methods, strategies to preempt of attackers, malware distribution, decentralized information storage, read only disk partitions, and physical security keys/passworldless accounts.

Privacy

Privacy

People are not property, but our personal information is being treated like property – being bought and sold from one company to another. With more and more websites collecting some sort of information from us, I think it's time for a change.

I believe our information should be private unless there is a valid reason for it to be public. Software should be designed with privacy in mind, rather than as an afterthought. Let's make privacy the default, rather than an option!

GUI 2048

Human Interface Devices

Defined as anything that a human uses to interact with a computer, such as a touch screen, mouse, keyboard, headset, webcam, microphone, speaker, and more.

Since people are becoming increasingly reliant on technology – some spend hours in front of computers every day – I'm interested in how we can make these devices more ergonomic so that people are confortable for extended sessions in front of the computer and reduce the likelihood of RSI or carpal tunnel syndrome.

CS Education

Computer Science Education

My high school lacked computer science curriculum of any sort – I was only able to take computer science courses in a traditional classroom setting once I got to college. This experience made me want to get computer science curriculum into more schools and get more students interested in the field. I'm also interested in improving education at the college level.

I have taken two classes related to computer science education at UCSD; you can see my portfolio here.

FOSS

Free and Open Source Software

With open source, many contributors can work on the project meaning that the best minds are free to work on the project. This translates to more efficient, more secure, more reliable code, with more features than closed source counterparts.

The transparency achieved by making the source code publicly available means the end user is free to look through the code so that perse can look at how the program works make sure there isn't any malicious code.
