Important People

    Key Figures:

  • Linus Torvalds

    Personal Site: https://www.cs.helsinki.fi/u/torvalds
    GitHub https://github.com/torvalds

    Advocate for open-source software and creator of the Linux kernel and Git software version control system. Linux is used on billions of devices ranging from smartphones and personal computers to super-computers and data servers. Git is the most popular version control system and is used extensively in industry.

  • Richard M. Stallman (RMS)

    Personal Site: https://stallman.org
    Stallman's Adventures: https://rms.sexy

    Free software advocate, Head of the GNU Project, and author of the GNU General Public License (GNU GPL) – the most widely used free software license. Actively campaigns against software patents, activation keys, proprietary standards, and more things that take away users' freedom.


    • Researchers:

  • Leo Porter

    https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~leporter

    Associate Teaching Professor of Computer Science at UCSD and advocate of Peer Instruction. Leo's research interests include computer science education and computer architecture. He teaches introductory programming, computer organization, and computer architecture.

  • Steven Swanson

    http://swanson.ucsd.edu

    Professor of Computer Science at UCSD and director of the Non-volatile Systems Laboratory. Steven's research interests include architecture, security, and reliablity of solid-state storage. He teaches computer architecture and a quadcopter lab.

  • Shachar Lovett

    https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~slovett/home.html

    Associate Professor of Computer Science at UCSD and member of the Theory of Computation Group. Shachar's research interests include computational complexity, randomness and pseudo-randomness, coding theory, and more. He primarily teaches computational theory classes.

  • Stefan Savage

    http://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~savage/

    Professor of Computer Science at UCSD and member of the Systems and Networking Group renowned for his research in computer security. Stefan's research interests include email spam, DDoS attacks, network worms, and more. He primarily teaches computer security classes.

  • Taylor Berg-Kirkpatrick

    https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~tberg/ http://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~savage/

    Assistant Professor of Computer Science at UCSD and head of the Berg Lab. Taylor's research interests are primarily in machine learning, specifically natural language processing. He primarily teaches machine learning classes.


Companies and Organizations

  • Red Hat

    https://www.redhat.com/en

    Provider of open-source software to enterprise and creators of Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL). In addition to RHEL, they are also affiliated with The Fedora Project and The CentOS Project, which are the creators of Fedora Linux and CentOS Linux, respectively.

  • Mozilla

    https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/exp

    Creators of the Firefox web browser, Thunderbird email client, and advocates for free and open-source software, user privacy, and user freedom. Their mission is to "ensure the Internet is a global public resource, open and accessible to all. An Internet that truly puts people first, where individuals can shape their own experience and are empowered, safe and independent."

  • The Linux Foundation

    https://www.linuxfoundation.org

    A foundation sponsored by corporate members to support "the creation of sustainable open source ecosystems by providing financial and intellectual resources, infrastructure, services, events, and training." Members include Google, Microsoft, Cisco, Samsung, Qualcomm, Toyota, Adobe, AMD, and more. The Foundation is the largest shared technology investment and aims to be a neutral governing body.

    You can see a list of all of its members here.

  • Raspberry Pi Foundation

    https://www.raspberrypi.org

    Creators of the Raspberry Pi, a small computer that costs as little as $5. All Raspberry Pi's use ARM processors and rely on micro SD cards for storage. The organization's goal is to "put the power of computing and digital making into the hands of people all over the world. We do this so that more people are able to harness the power of computing and digital technologies for work, to solve problems that matter to them, and to express themselves creatively."
