About Mark:

Mark is an undergraduate student at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD) pursuing a B.S. in computer science and a minor in business, likely graduating in June 2021.

One of his earliest memories is getting a Compaq computer for Christmas and playing a fire-fighting simulation game when he was four years old. Although he no longers want to be a firefighter, the fascination with technology has stuck with him since. He has always been fascinated about how things work and would often take apart LEGO sets and use the pieces to build something else. As he progressed through school, he knew he wanted to be a computer scientist.

Before he became a UCSD student, his family members would always ask for his help when they have a technology-related issue – and they still do to this day! He also did graphic design for a non-profit, and took several online courses on app development. Since he became a UCSD student, several more activities have further stimulated his interest in computer science. You can see some of them on the homepage. In addition to those activities, he also volunteers at an elementary school, where he has been instrumental in getting more young students interested in computer science.

His strong background in business administration has given him the opportunity to manage several large teams in the past – most notably the You See Housing team in a software engineering class. His interests include user interface design, cybersecurity, and computer simulation. He is a proponent of free and open-source software and privacy of information. You can read more about his interests on the homepage. One day, he hopes to use what he has learned to start up his own company that would help him to change society for the better.

When he's not busy with school work, he enjoys swimming, cycling, surfing, listening to music, drawing, building with LEGO, watching videos on YouTube, playing with his cat, and talking about himself in the third person.
